THE CONOR THORN SERIES

Glenn Dyer is the author of the Conor Thorn series—five WWII espionage thrillers praised for their meticulous research, breakneck pacing, and authentic period detail. Endorsed by Steve Berry, Jack Carr, and Chris Wallace, his novels have been called "a perfect fit for fans of Alan Furst."

Each book draws on declassified intelligence records, firsthand wartime accounts, and years of archival research—placing readers at the heart of history's most dangerous secrets.

IN HIS OWN WORDS

"The most dramatic events in WWII happened in the shadows—and that's exactly where I want to take readers."

Glenn Dyer's passion for WWII intelligence history began long before his writing career. A lifelong student of the conflict's shadow operations—from OSS field agents to British double-cross networks—he spent years researching the forgotten operatives, betrayals, and narrow escapes that shaped the war's outcome.

That research became the foundation for Conor Thorn: an American OSS officer operating in occupied Europe, where every ally is a potential threat and every mission could be the last. Dyer's commitment to historical accuracy means every operation, location, and piece of tradecraft in the novels has roots in the real historical record.

WRITING PHILOSOPHY

History Is the Thriller

The men and women of WWII's intelligence services lived inside one of history's most extraordinary thrillers—they just didn't know how it ended. My job is to put readers alongside them, feeling every decision as if the outcome is still uncertain.

A great historical thriller has to earn its tension honestly. That means the history has to be right, the tradecraft has to be real, and the stakes have to feel genuine. Conor Thorn operates in the actual shadow world of wartime intelligence—every place, person, and operation is grounded in the historical record.
